Story

I wrote this little "excercise book" initially only for myself. I usually learn new things by doing as much excercise as I can. Obviously you can find lots of excercises related to regex on the internet, even you can make same on your own, but finding a complete collection of excercises builded some kind of logical order is not easy. That is why I created this.

Notes

  • I used Mastering Regular Expressions Third Edition by Jeffrey E. F. Friedl. The excercise book follows the order of the book
  • The book uses egrep. For the difference between grep, egrep and fgrep please follow this link
  • Because of deprecating egrep, I used grep -E everywhere, you should do that too
  • I used Linux (Arch Linux) for the excercises, I do not know PowerShell, they may or may not work on Windows
  • Bash was my shell interpreter. I recommend you to make an alies for the earlier mentioned 'grep -E' command:
    • Put this to your ~/.bash_profile or ~/.bashrc (w/o single quotes): 'alias egc="grep -E --color"' and issue either source ~/.bash_profile or source ~/.bashrc command (or you can log out and log in again)
